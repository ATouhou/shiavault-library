The Rights of Allah against oneself
===================================

1. The Greatest Right of Allah
------------------------------

The greatest right of God against you is that you worship Him without
associating anything with Him. When you do that with sincerity
(*ikhlas*), He has made it binding upon Himself to give you sufficiency
in the affair of this world and the next.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
حقوق الله
  </p>
</blockquote>

> 1 . حق الله الأكبر

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
فأَمَّا حَقُّ اللهِ الأَكْبَرُ فَإنَّكَ تَعْبُدُهُ لا تُشْرِكُ بهِ
شَيْئاً، فَإذَا فَعَلْتَ ذَلِكَ بإخلاصٍ جَعَلَ لَكَ عَلَى نَفْسِهِ
أَنْ يَكفِيَكَ أَمْرَ الدُّنْيَا وَالآخِرَةِ وَيَحْفَظَ لَكَ مَا
تُحِبُّ مِنْهَما.
  </p>
</blockquote>

2) The Right of your self
-------------------------

The right of your self (*nafs*) against you is that you employ it in
obeying God; then you deliver to your tongue its right, to your hearing
its right, to your sight its right, to your hand its right, to your leg
its right, to your stomach its right, to your private part its right,
and you seek help from God in all that.

> 2. حق النفس

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ نَفْسِكَ عَلَيْكَ فَأَنْ تَسْتَوْفِيَهَا فِي طَاعَةِ
اللهِ فَتُؤَدّي إلَى لِسَانِكَ حَقَّهُ وَإلَى سَمْعِك حَقَّهُ وَإلَى
بَصَرِكَ حَقّهُ وَإلَى يَدِكَ حَقَّهَا وَإلَى رِجْلِك حَقَّهَا وَإلَى
بَطْنِكَ حَقَّهُ وَإلَى فَرْجِكَ حَقَّهُ وَتَسْتَعِينَ باللهِ عَلَى
ذَلِك.
  </p>
</blockquote>

3) The Right of the Tongue
--------------------------

The right of the tongue is that you consider it too noble for obscenity,
accustom it to good, refrain from any meddling in which there is nothing
to be gained, express kindness to the people, and speak well concerning
them.

> 3. حق اللسان

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ اللّسَان فَإكْرَامُهُ عَنِ الْخَنَى وتَعْوِيدُهُ
الْخَيْرِ وَ تَرك الْفُضُول الَّتي لا فائِدَةِ لَها و البِرُّ
بالنَّاسِ و حُسْن القَولِ فيهم.
  </p>
</blockquote>

4) The Right of the Hearing
---------------------------

The right of hearing is to keep it pure from listening to backbiting
(*ghiba*) and listening to that to which it is unlawful to listen.

> 4. حق السمع

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ السَّمْعِ فَتَنْزِيهُهُ عَنْ سَماع الغيبةِ و سَماعِها
لا يحِلّ سَماعه.
  </p>
</blockquote>

5) The Right of the Sight
-------------------------

The right of sight is that you lower it before everything which is
unlawful to you and that you take heed whenever you look at anything.
[^1]

> 5. حق البصر

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ بَصَرِكَ أن تُغْمِضَهُ عَمَّا لا يَحِلُّ لَكَ و
تَعْتَبِر بالنَّظَرِ بِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

6) The Right of the Hand
------------------------

The right of your hand is that you stretch it not toward that which is
unlawful to you.

> 6. حق اليد

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ يَدِكَ فَأَنْ لا تَبْسُطَهَا إلَى مَا لا يَحِلُّ لَكَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

7) The Right of the Legs
------------------------

The right of your two legs is that you walk not with them toward that
which is unlawful to you. You have no escape from standing upon the
narrow bridge (*al-sirat* [over hell]), so you should see to it that
your legs do not slip and cause you to fall into the Fire.

> 7. حق الرجلين

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ رِجْلَيْكَ فَأَنْ لا تَمْشِي بهِمَا إلَى مَا لا يَحِلُّ
لَكَ فيهما، تقف على الصراط فانظر أن لا تزل بك فتتردى في النار.
  </p>
</blockquote>

8) The Right of the Stomach
---------------------------

The right of your stomach is that you make it not into a container for
that which is unlawful to you and you eat no more than your fill
(*shib'*).

> 8. حق البطن

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ بَطْنِكَ فَأَنْ لا تَجْعَلَهُ وِعَاءً لِلْحَرَامِ وَلا
تَزيد على الشَّبْعَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

9) The Right of the Private part
--------------------------------

The right of your private part (*farj*) is that you protect it from
fornication and guard it against being looked upon.

> 9. حق الفرج

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَأَمَّا حَقُّ فَرْجِكَ انْ تُحْصِنَهُ عَن الْزِّنا و تَحْفِظَهَ مِن
أنْ يُنْظَر الَيْهِ. .
  </p>
</blockquote>

[^1]: Allusion to 59:2: Therefore take heed, you who have eyes!
