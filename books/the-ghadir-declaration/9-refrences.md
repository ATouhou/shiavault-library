The words of this tradition as recorded in*as-Sunnah* (pp.600,601 \#
1351) by Ibn Abī ‘Āsim are as follows:

<p dir="rtl">
من كنت وليه فعلي وليه.
</p>

*Who has me as his guardian has ‘Alī as his guardian.*

The tradition narrated by Nasā’ī has a sound chain of succession.

Dhahabī graded Hākim’s narrated tradition as*sahīh* (sound).

Haythamī has said that it has been narrated by Ahmad and Tabarānī and
its men are those of*sahīh* (sound) *hadīth* except Abū Balj Farāzī
while he is *thiqah* (trustworthy).

[33]. Hindī, while relating it in*Kanz-ul-‘ummāl* (11:608 \# 32945), has

in*Fadā’il-us-sahābah* on the authority of Zayd bin Arqam (RA) and Barā’
bin al-‘Āzib (RA).

‘Asqalānī also narrated it in*al-Isābah fī tamyīz-is-sahābah* (4:328).

in*al-Musnad* (1:88); and Haythamī has copied it in *Majma‘-uz-zawā’id*
(9:106) and declared its men trustworthy ( *rijāluhū thiqah* ).
