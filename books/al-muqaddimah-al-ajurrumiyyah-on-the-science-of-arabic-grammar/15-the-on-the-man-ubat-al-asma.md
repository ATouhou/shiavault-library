(the Places of Naṣb of the Ism)

The Manṣūbāt[^63] are fifteen[^64] , and they are:

the Maf‘ūl bihī (direct object)

the Maṣdar (infinitive)

the Ẓarf Zamān (adverb of time)

the Ẓarf Makān (adverb of place)

the Ḥāl (denotative of state/condition)

the Tamyīz (specifier)

the Mustathnā (thing excepted/excluded)

the Ism of "لا"

the Munādā (vocative/thing or person called)

the Maf‘ūl min ajlihī (object of reason)

the Maf‘ūl ma‘ahū (object of accompaniment)

the Khabar of "كان"َ and its sisters

the Ism of "إنّ" and its sisters

the Tābi‘ of something that is Manṣūb, and it is four things: the Na‘t,
the ‘Aṭf, the Taukīd and the Badal.
