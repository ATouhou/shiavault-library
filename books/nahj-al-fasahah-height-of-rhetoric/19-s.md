=

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
تَحَفَّظُوْا مِنَ الاَرْضِ فَاِنَّهَا أُمُّكُمْ، وَاِنَّهُ لَيْسَ مِنْ
أَحَدٍ عَامِلٍ عَلَيْها خَيْرَاً أَوْ شَرَّاً اِلاّ وَهِيَ مُخْبِرَةٌ
بِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Safeguard the earth, for it is your mother who will report (to God) the
good or evil any one does on it.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الشَّيْطَانُ يَهُمُّ بِالواحِدِ وَالاِثْنَيْنِ، فَاِذا كانُوا ثَلاثَةً
لَمْ يَهُمَّ بِهِمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Satan tries to deceive one or two persons (when sitting together) but
leaves them as soon as they turn out to be three.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ما اسْتَفَادَ المُؤْمِنُ بَعْدَ تَقْوَى اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ خَيْرَاً
لَهُ مِنْ زَوْجَةٍ صَالِحَةٍ؛ إنْ أمَرَهَا أطَاعَتْهُ؛ وَإنْ نَظَرَ
اِلَيْهَا سَرَّتْهُ؛ وَإنْ أقْسَمَ عَلَيْهَا أبَرَّتْهُ؛ وَإنْ غَابَ
عَنْهَا نَصَحَتْهُ في نَفْسِهَا وَمَالِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Save the Almighty God, a believer does not have anything better than an
abstinent wife who obeys his orders, makes him cheerful when he casts a
look at her, observes his oath whenever he swears, and keeps his wealth
and her own modesty intact in his absence.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
قُوْا بِأَمْوالِكُمْ عَنْ أعْرَاضِكُمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Save your face by your wealth.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِتُّقُوا النَّارَ وَلَوْ بِشِقِّ تَمْرَةٍ؛ فَاِنْ لَمْ تَجِدُوا
فَبِكَلِمَةٍ طَيِّبَةٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Save yourself from Fire, even through half a date; or by good words, if
lacking the former.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
قُوْلُوْا خَيْرَاً تَغْنَمُوا وَاسْكُتُوْا عَنْ شَرٍّ تَسْلَمُوْا.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Say a good (word) to derive benefit, and avoid speaking evil words to be
safe.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
قُلْ أللَّهُمَّ اِنِّي أسْأَلُكَ نَفْسَاً مُطْمَئِنَّةً تُؤْمِنُ
بِلِقَائِكَ، وَتَرْضَى بِقَضَائِكَ، وَتَقْنَعُ بِعَطَائِكَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Say: O God! I beg You to bestow upon me a confident heart, believing in
Your visit (in the Last Day (, pleased with Your predestination and
content with Your blessings.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
قُلِ أللَّهُمَّ اجْعَلْ سَريرَتِي خَيْرَاً مِنْ عَلانِيَتِي، وَاجْعَلْ
عَلانِيَتِي صَالِحَةً. أللَّهُمَّ اِنِّي أسْأَلُكَ مِنْ صَالِحِ مَا
تُؤْتِي النَّاسَ مِنَ المَالِ وَالأهْلِ وَالوَلَدِ غَيْرِ الضَّالِّ
وَلا المُضِلِّ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Say: O God! make my inner self better than my outer self, and rectify
the latter. O God! I beg You to give me the good you have bestowed upon
people, of wealth, family and children that are neither deviated nor
deviating.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
صَلاةُ بِسِواكٍ أفْضَلُ مِنْ سَبْعِيْنَ صَلاةً بِغَيْرِ سِواكٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Saying prayer with one's teeth brushed weighs over seventy prayers said
with unbrushed teeth.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
العلماءُ أمَنَاءُ اللَّهِ عَلى خَلْقِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Scholars are God's trustees for people.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
العَالِمُ أَمِينُ اللَّهِ في الأرْضِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Scholars are God's trustees on earth.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
البَذاءُ شُؤْمُ؛ وَسُوْءُ المَلَكَةِ لُؤْمٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Scurrility incites enmity, and malice brings meanness.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أُطْلُبُوا الْعِلْمَ وَلَوْ بِالصِّينِ؛ فَاِنَّ طَلَبَ الْعِلْمِ
فَرِيضَةٌ عَلى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ، اِنَّ الْمَلآئِكَةَ تَضَعُ أَجْنِحَتَها
لِطَالِبِ الْعِلْمِ رِضَاً بِمَا يَطْلُبُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Search for knowledge even (if it is to be acquired) in China, for
seeking knowledge is a precept for all Muslims, and angels spread their
wings for the seekers of knowledge (to sit on) and derive pleasure from
what they search for.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أُطْلُبُوا العِلْمَ مِنَ الْمَهْدِ اِلَى اللَّحْدِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Search for lore from the birth shore to the grave door.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
حَصِّنُوْا أمْوالَكُمْ بِالزَّكاةِ، وَداوُوا مَرْضاكُمْ بِالصَّدَقَةِ،
وَأعِدُّوْا لِلْبَلاءِ الدُّعَاءَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Secure your properties by giving alms, cure your patients through
charity, and resort to praying to guard yourself against calamity.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الأمْنُ وَالعَافِيَةُ نِعْمَتَانِ مَغْبُوْنٌ فيهَا كَثِيْرٌ مِنَ
النَّاسِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Security and health are two (divine) blessings most people lose.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
بَيْنَ يَدَيِ السَّاعَةِ فِتَنٌ كَقِطَعِ اللَّيْلِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sedition will arise before the Hour, as gloomy as parts of dark nights.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
سَتَكُونُ فَتِنٌ يُصْبِحُ الرَّجُلُ فيها مُؤْمِنَاً، وَيُمْسِي
كافِرَاً، إلاّ مَنْ أحْيَاهُ اللَّهُ بِالعِلْمِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seditions will soon arise. When believers in the morning will turn out
to be disbelievers at night, save those whom God has enlivened their
hearts with knowledge.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أُنْظُرْ فيْ أَيِّ نِصَابٍ تَضَعُ وَلَدَكَ؛ فَاِنَّ اَلعِرْقَ دَسَّاسٌ
  </p>
</blockquote>

See where you place your child (cultivate your sperm),for nature leaves
its effect.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِلْتَمِسْ وَلَوْ خَاتَمَاً مِنْ حَدِيْدٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seek (something), even if it is an iron ring.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِلْتَمِسُوا الرِّزْقَ بِالنِّكَاحِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seek (your) sustenance through marriage.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِذا ابْتَغَيْتُمُ الْمَعْرُوفَ فَاطْلُبُوهُ عِنْدَ حِسَانِ
الْوُجُوهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seek good deeds with holders of good faces.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
عَلَيْكَ بِالعِلْمِ؛ فَاِنَّ العِلْمَ خَلِيْلُ المُؤْمِنِ، وَالحِلْمَ
وَزِيْرُهُ، وَالعَقْلَ دَلِيْلُهُ، وَالعَمَلَ قَيِّمُهُ، وَالرِّفْقَ
أبُوهُ، وَاللِّيْنَ أخُوهُ، وَالصَّبْرَ أمِيْرُ جُنُودِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seek knowledge, for it is a believer's friend, his minister being
forbearance, his guidance being intellect, his guardian being (good)
deeds, his father being moderateness, his brother being gentleness, and
the commander of his army being patience.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أُطْلُبُوا الرِّزْقَ في خَبَايَا الْاَرْضِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seek your sustenance in the hideouts of the earth.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
طَالِبُ العِلْمِ طَالِبُ الرَّحْمَةِ، طَالِبُ العِلْمِ رُكْنُ
الإسْلامِ وَيُعْطى أجْرُهُ مَعَ النَّبِيِّيْنَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seekers of knowledge are seekers of God's Mercy and pillars of Islam,
who will be rewarded by prophets.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
طَلَبُ العِلْمِ فَرِيْضَةٌ عَلى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ، وَواضِعُ العِلْمِ
عِنْدَ غَيْرِ أهْلِهِ كَمُقَلِّدِ الخَنَازِيْرِ الجَوْهَرَ
وَاللُؤْلُوءَ وَالذَّهَبَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seeking knowledge is a must for all Muslims and whoever issues forth his
knowledge to incompetent people is like unto the one who beautifies pigs
with jewels, pearls, and gold.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
طَلَبُ العِلْمِ فَرِيْضَةٌ عَلى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ؛ وَأنَّ طَالِبَ
العِلْمِ يَسْتَغْفِرُ لَهُ كُلُّ شَيْ‏ءٍ حَتَّى الحِيْتَانِ فِي
البَحْرِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seeking knowledge is a must for all Muslims; and everything, even fish
in seas, ask forgiveness for the seekers of knowledge.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
طَلَبُ العِلْمِ فَريضَةٌ عَلى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ وَمُسْلِمَةٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seeking knowledge is obligatory for all Muslims- men and women alike.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
مَنْ طَلَبَ العِلْمَ كَانَ كَفَّارَةً لِمَا مَضَى.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seeking knowledge is the atonement of one's past sins.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِنَّ الْعُجْبَ لَيُحْبِطُ عَمَلَ سَبْعِيْنَ سَنَةٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Selfishness ruins one's worship of seventy years.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
لَيْسَ لِلدِّيْنِ رُوَّادٌ إلاّ القَضَاءَ وَالوَفَاءَ وَالحَمْدَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sense of duty, fidelity and praising God stand at the peak of all
religious affairs.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
خِدْمَتُكَ زَوْجَتَكَ صَدَقَةٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Serving your wife is a kind of charity.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
صَلِّ صَلاةَ مُودِّعٍ كَأَنَّكَ تَراهُ؛ فَاِنْ كُنْتَ لا تَراهُ
فَاِنَّهُ يَراكَ، وَايْأَسْ مِمَّا في أيْدِي النَّاسِ تَعِشْ
غَنِيَّاً؛ وَاِيَّاكَ وَمَا يُعْتَذَرُ مِنْهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Set prayer humbly, as if you see God. If you do not see Him, He (surely)
sees you. Expect nothing from people to live free from want, and avoid
whatever requires your later pardoning.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
سَبْعَةٌ يُظِلُّهُمُ اللَّهُ تَحْتَ ظِلِّ عَرْشِهِ يَوْمَ لا ظِلَّ
إِلاّ ظِلُّهُ: رَجُلٌ قَلْبُهُ مُعَلَّقٌ بِالمَسَاجِدِ، وَرَجُلٌ
دَعَتْهُ امْرَأةٌ ذاتُ مَنْصَبٍ فَقَالَ: اِنِّي أَخَافُ اللَّهَ،
وَرَجُلانِ تَحَابَّا في اللَّهِ، وَرَجُلٌ غَضَّ عَيْنَهُ عَنْ
مَحَارِمِ اللَّهِ، وَعَيْنٌ حَرَسَتْ في سَبِيْلِ اللَّهِ، وَعَيْنٌ
بَكَتْ مِنْ خَشْيَةِ اللَّهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seven (groups of) people will be allowed (to rest) under the shade of
God's Throne in the day when there will be as shade but His: one who is
whole- heartedly fond of mosques, one who rejects the sensual request of
a high- rank woman for (his) fear of God, one who makes friends (with
people) for God's sake, one who controls his (her)eyes against the
unlawful, one who acts as a watchout in the way of God, and one who
weeps out of fearing God.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
بَيْنَ العَبْدِ وَالجَنَّةِ سَبْعُ عِقَابٍ؛ أَهْوَنُهَا المَوْتُ؛
وَأْصعَبُهَا الوُقُوْفُ بَيْنَ يَدَي اللَّهِ تَعَالى؛ اِذا تَعَلَّقَ
المَظْلوُمُونَ بِالظَّالِمِيْنَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seven events are there between a servant of God and Paradise, the
easiest one being death and the hardest, arriving at the presence of the
Exalted God, when the oppressed call the oppressors to account.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
سَبعةٌ يُظِلُّهُمُ اللَّهُ في ظِلِّهِ يَوْمَ لا ظِلَّ إلاّ ظِلُّهُ:
اِمَامٌ عَادِلٌ وَشَابٌّ نَشَأَ في عِبَادَةِ اللَّهِ، وَرَجُلٌ
قَلْبُهُ مُعَلَّقٌ بِالمَسْجِدِ، اِذا خَرَجَ مِنْهُ حَتَّى يَعُودَ
إلَيْهِ، وَرَجُلانِ تَحَابَّا في اللَّهِ فَاجْتَمَعَا عَلى ذلِكَ
وَافْتَرَقَا عَلَيْهِ، وَرَجُلٌ ذَكَرَ اللَّهَ خَالِيَاً فَفاضَتْ
عَيْنَاهُ، وَرَجُلٌ دَعَتْهُ امْرَأةٌ ذاتُ مَنْصَبٍ وَجَمَالٍ فَقَالَ:
اِنِّي أَخَافُ اللَّهَ رَبَّ العَالَمِيْنَ، وَرَجُلٌ تَصَدَّقَ
بِصَدَقَةٍ فَأَخْفَاهَا حَتَّى لا تَعْلَمَ شِمَالُهُ مَا تُنْفِقُ
يَمِيْنُهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seven persons will be allowed (to rest) under God's Throne in the day
when there will be no shade but His: a just leader, a youth grown at in
serving God, a man who whole- heartedly wishes to return to the mosque
whenever leaving it, a man who makes friends and breaks friendship for
God's sake, a man who remembers God in privacy and sheds tears, a man
who rejects the sensual request of a beautiful, high- rank woman out of
fearing God- the Lord of the worlds- and a man whose left hand knows not
what his right hand has hiddenly given in charity.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
بَيْنَ العَالِمِ وَالعَابِدِ سَبْعُونَ دَرَجَةً.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seventy ranks separate a learned man from a (simple) worshipper.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِنَّ الاَرْضَ لَتُنَادِي كُلُّ يَوْمٍ سَبْعِيْنَ مَرَّةً: يَا بَني
آدَمَ كُلُوا مَا شِئْتُمْ وَاشْتَهَيْتُمْ؛ فَوَاللَّهِ لَآكُلَنَّ
لُحُومَكُمْ وَجُلُودَكُمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Seventy times a day, the Earth calls out, "O human beings, eat whatever
you like and wish for, by God, I will eat your skin and flesh!

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
تَصَافَحُوْا يَذْهَبُ الغِلُّ عَنْ قُلُوبِكُمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shake hands (with each other) to make grudges leave your hearts.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
مِنْ تَمَامِ التَّحِيَّةِ الأخَذُ بِالْيَدِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shaking hands is the perfect form of salutation.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ألا أُخْبِرُكَ عَنْ مُلُوكِ الْجَنَّةِ؟ رَجُلٌ ضَعِيْفٌ مُسْتَضْعَفٌ
ذُو طِمْرَيْنِ لا يُؤْبَهُ لَهُ؛ لَوْ أَقْسَمَ عَلى اللَّهِ
لَأَبَرَّهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of one of the kings of Paradise? It is a weak,
oppressed and poorly- dressed person to whom nobody pays attention, but
whose swears come true when he swears by God for something to happen.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكَ بِأَهْلِ النَّار؟ كُلُّ جَعْظَرِيٍّ جَوَّاظٍ
مُسْتَكْبِرٍ جَمَّاعٍ مَنُوعٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of the dwellers of Hell? They are the proud, selfish,
arrogant, greedy and avaricious people.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِأَيْسَرِ الْعِبَادَةِ وَأَهْوَنِهَا عَلَى
الْبَدَنِ؟ الصَّمْتُ وَحُسْنُ الْخُلْقِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of the easiest acts of worship? - silence and
good-naturedness.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِنِسَائِكُمْ مِنْ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ؟ الْوَدُودُ
الْوَلُودُ الْعَئُودُ الَّتِي اِذا ظَلَمَتْ قَالَتْ: هذِه يَدِي في

Shall I inform you of the female dwellers of Paradise?. The faithful
women who give birth to a lot of children, who make peace before long,
and who tell their husbands immediately after a wrongdoing, "My very
hand is in yours, I will not sleep until you get pleased with me."

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكَ بِخَيْرِ مَا يَكْنِزُ الْمَرْءُ؟ الْمَرْأَةُ
الصَّالِحَةُ؛ اِذا نَظَرَ اِلَيْهَا سَرَّتْهُ؛ وَاِذا أَمَرَهَا

</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of the most precious treasure of a man? It is a wife
who makes him cheerful when he casts a glance at her, who obeys his
orders, and who keeps his trusts intact in his absence.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أَدُلُّكُمْ عَلى أَشَدِّكُمْ؟ أَمْلَكُكُمْ عِنْدَ الْغَضَبِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of the strongest people? those who can reign their
rage better than others.

<blockquote dir="rtl">

أُنْبِئُكَ بِشَرٍّ مِنْ هذا؟ مَنْ بَاعَ آخِرَتَهُ بِدُنْيا غَيْرِهِ،
ألا أُنْبِئُكَ بِشَرٍّ مِنْ هذا؟ مَنْ أَكَلَ الدُّنْيا بِالدِّيْنِ.

Shall I inform you of the worst of all people?. Those who eat food
lonely, give nothing to anybody, travel alone and hit their servants.
Shall I inform you of the one who is worse than them? - - those whose
evil people are worried about and in whom no goodness can be found out.
Shall I inform you of those who are (even) worse than them? - - those
who barter their eternal life for others' worldly life. Shall I inform
you of those who are (even) worse? - - those who sacrifice religion to
enjoy the world.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِمَنْ يَحْرُمُ عَلَيْهِ النَّارُ غَدَاً؟ عَلى كُلِّ
هَيِّنٍ لَيِّنٍ قَرِيْبٍ سَهْلٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of those for whom the Fire will be banned Tomorrow?
The gentle, tender- hearted and easy- going people who get sweet on
people.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ألا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِأَفْضَلَ مِنْ دَرَجَةِ الصِّيَامِ؛ الصَّلاةِ؛
وَالصَّدَقَةِ؟ صَلاحُ ذاتِ الْبَيْنِ؛ فَاِنَّ فَسَادَ ذاتِ الْبَيْنِ
هِيَ الْحَالِقَةُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of what is better than fasting, prayer and charity?
It is making it up among people, for conflict among them results in
annihilation.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُخْبِرُكُمْ بِخَيْرِكُمْ مِنْ شَرِّكُمْ؟ خَيْرُكُمْ مِنْ يُرْجَى
خَيْرُهُ وَيُؤْمَنُ شَرُّهُ؛ وَشَرُّكُمْ مَنْ لا يُرْجَى خَيْرُهُ وَلا
يُؤْمَنُ شَرُّهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I inform you of your worst and best ones? The best of you is the
one whose good you can be hopeful about and from whose evil you can find
a way out, and the worst of you is the one whose good you cannot be
hopeful about and from whose evil you cannot find a way out.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلا أُعَلِّمُكَ خَصَلاتٍ يَنْفَعُكَ اللَّهُ تَعَالى بِهِنَّ؟ عَلَيْكَ
بِالْعِلْمِ فَاِنَّ الْعِلْمَ خَلِيلُ الْمُؤْمِنِ؛ وَالْحِلْمَ
وَزيرُهُ؛ وَالْعَقْلَ دَلِيلُهُ؛ وَالْعَمَلَ قَيِّمُهُ؛ وَالرِّفْقَ
أَبُوُهُ؛ وَاللِّيْنَ أَخُوهُ؛ وَالصَّبْرَ أَميرُ جُنُودِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shall I teach you some virtues by which the Exalted God makes you
profit? - acquire knowledge, for it is a friend of a believer,
forbearance being his counselor, wisdom his guide, action his guardian,
moderateness his father, leniency his brother and patience, the
commander of his army.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
كُلُوْا جَمِيْعَاً وَلا تَفَرَّقُوْا، فَاِنَّ طَعَامَ الوَاحِدِ
يَكْفِي الإثْنَيْنِ، وَطَعَامُ الإثْنَيْنِ يَكْفِي الثَّلاثَةَ
وَالأرْبَعَةَ. كُلُوْا جَمِيْعَاً وَلا تَفَرَّقُوا، فَاِنَّ البَرَكَةَ
مَعَ الجَمَاعَةِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Share your food with each other and be not divided among yourselves, for
the food of one person is sufficient for two and that of two persons is
enough for three. Share your food with each other and be not divided
among yourselves, for blessing is a company to community.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وَاكِلِي ضَيْفَكِ، فَاِنَّ الضَّيْفَ يَسْتَحْيي أنْ يَأْكُلَ وَحْدَهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Share your guest in food, for he may be shy to eat alone.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الرَّمْيُ خَيْرُ مَا لَهَوْتُمْ بِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Shooting is the best hobby for you.

secular and thank God for His grace, God

will consider him patient and grateful. Conversely, God will neither

بِالْمَعْرُوفِ وَنَهْيُكَ عَنِ المُنْكَرِ صَدَقَةٌ، وَاِرْشَادُكَ
الرَّجُلَ في أَرْضِ الضَّلالِ لَكَ صَدَقَةٌ، وَاِمَاطَتُكَ الحَجَرَ
وَالشَّوْكَ وَالعَظْمَ عَنِ الطَّرِيْقِ لَكَ صَدَقَةٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Smiling at your brother (in faith), enjoining good and forbidding evil,
guiding the lost ones and removing stones, thorns and bones from
(people's) path, are all different forms of charity.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَنْتُمْ عَلى بَيِّنَةٍ مِنْ رَبِّكُمْ مَا لَمْ تَظْهَرْ مِنْكُمْ
سَكْرَتَانِ سَكْرَةُ الْجَهْلِ وَسَكْرَةُ حُبِّ الدُّنْيا.
  </p>
</blockquote>

So long as the drunkenness of ignorance and that of the love of the
world do not appear in you, you can be sure about your Lord.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
مازَالَ جِبْرِيْلُ يُوْصِيْنِي بِالْجَارِ حَتَّى ظَنَنْتُ أنَّهُ
سَيُوَرِّثُهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

So many times did Gabriel recommend to me to observe neighbors' rights
that I came to think of them inheriting each other.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
خُيِّرَ سُلَيَْمانُ بَيْنَ المَالِ وَالمُلْكِ وَالعِلْمِ، فَاخْتَارَ
العِلْمَ، فَاُعْطِيَ المُلْكَ وَالمَالَ لِاخْتِيَارِهِ العِلْمَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Solomon was given the option to choose one among wealth, rule and
knowledge. He chose knowledge and was, as a result, bestowed upon the
other two.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
يَبْصُرُ أحَدُكُمْ القَذَى في عَيْنِ أخِيْهِ، وَيَدَعُ الجِذْعَ في
عَيْنِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Some of you can detect a tiny thorn in your brother's eyes, but not a
tree branch in your own.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ذَنْبٌ لا يُغْفَرُ وَذَنْبٌ لا يُتْرَكُ وَذَنْبٌ يُغْفَرُ. فَأَمَّا
الَّذِي لا يُغْفَرُ فَالشِّرْكُ بِاللَّهِ. وَأمَّا الَّذِي يُغْفَرُ
فَذَنْبُ العَبْدِ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَ اللَّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ. وَأمَّا
الَّذِي لا يُتْرَكُ فَظُلْمُ العِبَادِ بَعْضُهُمْ بَعْضَاً.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Some sins are irremissible, some are not left of account, and some are
forgiven, i.e. taking partners with God, oppressing one another, and
one's deeds known only to him and the Exalted God respectively.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ذَنْبٌ يُغْفَرُ وَذَنْبٌ لا يُغْفَرُ وَذَنْبٌ يُجَازى بِهِ. فَأَمَّا
الذَّنْبُ الَّذِي لا يُغْفَرُ فَالشِّرْكُ بِاللَّهِ. وَأمَّا الذَّنْبُ
الَّذِي يُغْفَرُ فَعَمَلُكَ بَيْنَكَ وَبَيْنَ رَبِّكَ. وَأمَّا
الذَّنْبُ الَّذِي يُجَازى بِهِ فَظُلْمُكَ أخَاكَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Some sins are remissible, some are irremissible and some punishable,
i.e. your deeds known to you and God, taking partners with God and
oppressing your brethren respectively.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
كُلُّ شَيْ‏ءٍ فَضَلَ عَنْ ظِلِّ بَيْتٍ وَجِلْفِ الخُبْزِ وَثَوْبٍ
يُوَارِي عَوْرَةَ الرَّجُلِ وَالمَاءِ لَمْ يَكُنْ لاِبْنِ آدَمَ فِيْهِ
حَقٌّ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sons of Adam are entitled to no more than the shade of a house, a dry
loaf of bread and a piece of cloth to cover their privy parts.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ما عَمِلَ ابْنُ آدَمَ شَيْئَاً أفْضَلَ مِنَ الصَّلاةِ وَصَلاحِ ذَاتِ
البَيْنِ وَخُلْقٍ حَسَنٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sons of Adam do not have any deed better than saying prayer, reconciling
people and behaving well.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
ما مِنْ سَاعَةٍ تَمُرُّ بِابْنِ آدَمَ لَمْ يَذْكُرِ اللَّهَ فيها إلاّ
حَسَرَ عَلَيْهَا يَوْمَ القِيَامَةِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sons of Adam will in the Day of Judgment be surely regretful for times
of forgetting God in this world.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الأرْوَاحُ جُنُوْدٌ مُجَنَّدَةٌ فَمَا تَعَارَفَ مِنْهَا ائْتَلَفَ؛
وَمَا تَنَاكَرَ مِنْهَا اخْتَلَفَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Souls are like disciplined armies- familiar souls are allied and
unfamiliar ones, aliens.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
عَلَيْكَ بِحُسْنِ الكَلامِ وَبَذْلِ الطَّعَامِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Speak well and feed people.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَنْفِقِي وَلا تُحْصِي فَيُحْصِي اللَّهُ عَلَيْكِ وَلا تُوعِي فَيُوعِي
اللَّهُ عَلَيْكِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Spend (in the way of God) but do not count the amount, for God will then
count (your sins); and do not be stingy (towards people), for God will
do the same to you.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِبْدَأْ بِنَفْسِكَ فَتَصَدَّقْ عَلَيْها؛ فَاِنْ فَضَلَ شَيْى‏ءٌ
فَلِأهْلِكَ؛ فَاِنْ فَضَلَ شَيْى‏ءٌ عَنْ أَهْلِكَ فَلِذِي
قَرَابَتِكَ؛فَاِنْ فَضَلَ عَنْ ذي قَرابَتِكَ شَيْى‏ءٌ، فَهكَذا وَهكَذا
اِبْدَأْ بِمَنْ تَعُولُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Start being charitable to yourself and then proceed to your household,
your relatives and others respectively. Again, begin it from your
household.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
سَلامَةُ الرَّجُلِ مِنَ الفِتْنَةِ أنْ يَلْزَمَ بَيْتَهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Staying at home saves man from seditions.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
المِعْدَةُ بَيْتُ الدَّاءِ؛ وَالحِمْيَةُ رَأْسُ كُلِّ دَواءٍ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Stomach is the home (source) of all illness, and abstinence from
gluttony is the best drug.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
تَدارَكُوْا الهُمُومَ وَالغُمُومَ بِالصَّدَقَاتِ؛ يَكْشِفِ اللَّهُ
تَعَالى ضُرَّكُمْ وَيَنْصُرْكُمْ عَلى عَدُوِّكُمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Stop griefs and sorrows through charity so that the Exalted God save you
from them and grant you victory over enemies.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَشِيْدُوا النِّكَاحَ وَأَعْلِنُوهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Strengthen marriage contract and make it known to people.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
صِلُوْا الَّذِي بَيْنَكُمْ وَبَيْنَهُ قَرَابَةٌ بِكَثْرَةِ ذِكْرِكُمْ
اِيَّاهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Strengthen ties of kinship by remembering your family frequently.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِسْتَعِيْنُوا عَلَى النِّسَاءِ بِالْعُرَى فَاِنَّ اِحْدَاهُنَّ اِذا
كَثُرَتْ ثِيَابُهَا؛ وَأَحْسَنَتْ زِينَتَهَا أَعْجَبَهَا الْخُرُوجُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Strengthen your control of women through (providing them with)
insufficient clothing, for when a woman comes to have excessive dresses
and ornaments, she tends to roam outside home.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الشَّدِيْدُ مَنْ غَلَبَ نَفْسَهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Strong is the one who has self- control.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَلْزِمُوا الْجِهَادَ تَصِحُّوا وَتَسْتَغْنُوا.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Struggle (in the path of Deity), healthy and needless to be.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
جاهِدُوْا المُشْرِكِيْنَ بِأَمْوالِكُمْ وَأَنْفُسِكُمْ
وَأَلْسِنَتِكُمْ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Struggle against polytheists through your wealth and souls and words.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
جاهِدُوْا أَهْواءَكُمْ تَمْلِكُوْا أَنْفُسَكُمْ
  </p>
</blockquote>

Struggle against your passions to gain control over your souls.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
أَقْرَبُ الْعَمَلِ اِلَى اللَّهِ الْجِهَادُ في سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَلا
يُقَارِبُهُ شَيْى‏ءٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Struggling in the way of God is the nearest of all deeds to Him, and
nothing is equal to it.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الخُرْقُ شُؤْمٌ وَالرِّفْقُ يُمْنٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Stupidity is inauspicious and moderateness is auspicious (in nature).

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
كَفَى بِالمَوْتِ وَاعِظَاً. وَكَفَى بِاليَقِيْنِ غِنَىً
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suffice it for death to admonish and for certainty to (make man)
needless.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
كَفَى بِالمَوْتِ مُزَهِّدَاً في الدُّنْيَا، وَمُرَغِّبَاً في
الآخِرَةِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suffice it for death to decline your love for the world and to incline
you towards eternity.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
كَفَى بِالدَّهْرِ وَاعِظَاً وَبِالمَوْتِ مُفَرِّقَاً.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suffice it for time to advise and for death to separate.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
اِسْتَعِيْنُوا عَلى أُمورِكُمْ بِالْكِتْمَانِ؛ فَاِنَّ كُلَّ ذِي
نِعْمَةٍ مَحْسُودٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Support your affairs through secrecy, for all holders of blessings are
subject to others' jealousy.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
دِيْنَارٌ أنْفَقْتَهُ فِي سَبِيْلِ اللَّهِ وَدِيْنَارٌ أنْفَقْتَهُ فِي
رَقَبَةٍ وَدِيْنَارٌ تَصَدَّقْتَ بِهِ عَلى مِسْكِيْنٍ وَدِيْنَارٌ
أنْفَقْتَهُ عَلى أهْلِكَ. أعْظَمُهَا أجْرَاً الَّذِي أنْفَقْتَهُ عَلى
أهْلِكَ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suppose you have given a Dinar in the way of God, another to free a
slave, another in charity to the poor, and still another to settle the
needs of your household. The last one is worthy of more reward than
others.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
عَجِبْتُ لِغَافِلٍ وَلا يُغْفَلُ عَنْهُ، عَجِبْتُ لِمُؤمِّلِ دُنْيَا
وَالمَوْتُ يَطْلُبهُ، وَعَجِبْتُ لِضَاحِكٍ مِلْأَ فَمِهِ وَلا يَدْرِي
أرَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ أمْ أسْخَطَهُ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Surprised am I of the negligent who is not to be neglected, and of the
seeker of the (material) world who is sought by death, and of the one
who laughs boisterously but knows not weather God is pleased or
displeased with him.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
تَجَافَوْا عَنْ عُقُوبَةِ ذَوِي المُرُوَّةِ ما لَمْ تَكُنْ حَدَّاً
مِنْ حُدُودِ اللَّهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suspend the punishment of Merciful people, unless they are condemned to
divinely- ordered penalties.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الحَزْمُ سُوْءُ الظَّنِّ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Suspicion is far- sightedness.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الرِّزْقُ إلى بَيْتٍ فِيْهِ سَخَاءٌ أسْرَعُ مِنَ الشَّفْرَةِ إلى
سَنَامِ البَعِيْرِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sustenance reaches a generous family faster than a knife hits a camel's
hump.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الرِّزْقُ أشَدُّ طَلَبَاً لِلْعَبْدِ مِنْ أجَلِهِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Sustenance seeks God's servants more than their death.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الحَلْفُ مَنْفِقَةٌ لِلْسِّلْعَةِ، مُمْحِقَةٌ لِلْبَرَكَةِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Swearing (in trade) makes goods sold, but blessing ruined.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
الحَلْفُ حِنْثٌ أوْ نَدَمٌ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Swearing ends either in violation or remorse.

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
السُّيُوفُ مَفَاتِيْحُ الجَنَّةِ.
  </p>
</blockquote>

Swords are keys to Paradise.
