Letter 66: To `Abdullah ibn al-`Abbas
=====================================

*(This letter has already been included with a different version)*

<blockquote dir="rtl">

  </p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
إلى عبدالله بن العباس رحمه الله
  </p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote dir="rtl">
  <p>
وقد مضى هذا الكتاب فيما تقدّم بخلاف هذه الرواية
  </p>
</blockquote>

And then, sometimes a person feels joyful about a thing which he was not
to miss in any case and feels grieved for a thing which was not to come
to him at all. Therefore, you should not regard the attainment of
pleasure and the satisfaction of the desire for revenge as the best
favour of this world, but it should be the putting off of the (flame of)
wrong and the revival of right. Your pleasure should be for what (good
acts) you have sent forward; your grief should be for what you are
leaving behind; and your worry should be about what is to befall after
death.
